EMERGENCE

The Ideology of Nazism revolves around Mein Kampf, a book Hitler wrote while in prison. He detailed his belief that Germany was to be an Aryan Nation, and so it must exterminate Jews and expand its Lebensraum into Slavic lands. He also rejected the treaty of Versailles, and beleived that power should be kept with one person in a state.

Creation of the Nazi party

Hitler joined the NSDAP, originally a Worker's Party, and became its leader in 1921. He wished to unite all Germans into one state and to gain land for Germany. He also wished to reject the Treaty of Versailles. The Nazi Party also had the SA, an armed force loyal to the party that silenced opponents. In 1923, the Nazis attempted a putsch in Munich, which failed when Police men fired at them. This is how Hitler got arrested and wrote Mein Kampf in prison.

Democratic Appeals

After the Putsch, Hitler decided that the best way into power was through the people themselves. He hoped to take control of the Reichstag democratically. This was because he was aware of the country's weak electoral system and wished to exploit it. He also wished to appeal to the German people in the midst of the Great depression. After campaigning and appealing to people as their way to restore Germany, Hitler managed to end up as Chancellor.
